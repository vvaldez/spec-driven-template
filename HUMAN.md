# HUMAN.md

## For the Human, Not the Models
This file is primarily for you, the human operator. AI assistants should normally ignore this file unless you explicitly ask them to use it. Treat this as a place to record your own preferences, constraints, and notes to your future self.

## About Me (The Human Operator)
Write a short description of yourself, your background, and what motivates you to use this template.

## My Constraints and Energy / Time Patterns
Capture practical realities that matter for planning, such as:
- How much time you typically have per day or week.
- When you have the most focus or least energy.
- External constraints (work, family, environment).

## How I Prefer to Work With Models
Explain how you want AI assistants to behave, for example:
- How directive or exploratory you want them to be.
- When you want more detail versus concise answers.
- How much you want them to challenge your assumptions.

## My Risk Tolerance and Safety Lines
Describe your comfort level with:
- Experimenting with new tools or patterns.
- Sharing partial context with third-party services.
- Changing specs versus locking them down.

## When I Will Create a Real Implementation Repository
Define your personal criteria for when to move from planning to coding, for example:
- “I have a stable enough spec for a first slice.”
- “I have identified major risks and how to handle them.”
- “I have the time and energy to maintain an actual codebase.”

When these conditions are met, you can:
- Create a separate implementation repository.
- Copy over selected specs from this template.
- Keep this repo as a clean planning and reference space.

## Notes to Future Me
Leave short, honest notes that you want your future self to see when revisiting this project. This might include reminders about why you paused, what you found fun, or what you want to try next time.
